üß≠Introduction

This guide is designed for Bangladeshi students, athletes, and fitness enthusiasts who want to track calories and macros accurately using local foods, without expensive tools.

It covers raw vs. cooked conversions, common household measurements, affordable meal-tracking strategies, and complete meal breakdowns suited to your lifestyle and budget.

Understanding how food weight changes during cooking is key.

For example:
  • 35g raw masoor dal ‚Üí ~100g cooked dal soup
  • 50g raw rice ‚Üí ~150g cooked rice
  • 100g raw chicken breast ‚Üí ~80g cooked chicken
Note: The tables below are based on local food averages compiled from authoritative sources including the Bangladesh Food Composition Table and USDA databases.

üì±How to Track Your Daily Intake with Apps

Using the right tracking app makes monitoring your carbs, protein, and fats much easier. Here are the best options for Bangladeshi foods:

Recommended Apps (Ranked by Bangladesh Food Database)

1. MyFitnessPal (Free, Premium: ~$50/year)

  • Largest food database globally
  • Search "masoor dal raw" (35g) OR "lentil soup cooked" (100g) ‚Äì both give accurate results
  • Has some Bangladeshi foods but may need manual entries for local dishes
  • Tip: Create custom meals for common Bengali dishes (e.g., "My Dal Bhat") to save time
  • Available on Android, iOS, and web

2. Cronometer (Free, Gold: $50/year)

  • MOST ACCURATE for micronutrients (vitamins, minerals)
  • Best for serious fitness tracking
  • Smaller database but allows custom foods easily
  • Shows detailed nutrient breakdown beyond just macros
  • Available on Android, iOS, and web

3. FatSecret (Completely Free)

  • Simple and lightweight
  • Good barcode scanner
  • Community database includes some South Asian foods
  • Best for beginners on a budget
  • Available on Android, iOS, and web

4. Nutritionix Track (Free)

  • Clean interface, easy to use
  • Natural language input: type "2 rotis and 1 egg" and it calculates
  • Works well for mixed meals
  • Available on iOS and web

How to Track Bangladeshi Foods Accurately

Option 1: Search Generic Names

  • For dal: Search "lentils boiled" or "red lentils cooked" (100g)
  • For rice: Search "white rice cooked" (150g per fist)
  • For chicken: Search "chicken breast cooked" (80g per palm)
  • For roti: Search "whole wheat roti" or "chapati" (1 piece)

Option 2: Use USDA Database Terms

Most apps use USDA (US Department of Agriculture) data. Use these terms:

  • Masoor Dal ‚Üí "Lentils, red, cooked"
  • Moong Dal ‚Üí "Mung beans, cooked"
  • Chhola ‚Üí "Chickpeas, cooked"
  • Pangas ‚Üí "Fish, tilapia, cooked"
  • Rui ‚Üí "Fish, carp, cooked"

Option 3: Create Custom Foods (Recommended for Regular Meals)

Save time by creating your own entries:

  1. Go to "My Foods" or "Custom Foods" in your app
  2. Add "Masoor Dal (My Recipe)" with your exact cooking method
  3. Enter: 100g cooked = 120 kcal, 9g protein, 20g carbs, 1g fat
  4. Reuse this entry every time you eat dal

Option 4: Create Custom Meals (Best for Daily Eating)

Bundle common combinations:

  • "My Breakfast" = 2 rotis + 1 egg + 1 glass milk
  • "Lunch Dal Bhat" = Rice + Dal + Fish + Vegetables
  • "Dinner Roti Sabzi" = 2 rotis + Vegetable curry + Curd

Once saved, log the entire meal in one tap!

üí°Tips for Tracking Without a Scale

  • Use the same plate or bowl for consistency
  • Stick to either raw or cooked measurements ‚Äì don't mix both
  • Track meals in your chosen app or a simple notebook
  • Adjust portions slightly each week based on your bodyweight progress
  • Take photos of your meals for visual reference and consistency
  • Pre-measure your common foods once, then use visual cues after
